# Content Guide: Workplan Slide

These guidelines tier down from the slide craft rules in SKILL.md. They govern what goes on this slide, not how it looks. For visual spec, see `components/workplan/spec.md`.

---

## Purpose

The workplan slide answers: "What happens, when, and what do we deliver?" It translates the objectives into a concrete week-by-week plan. The reader should be able to scan this slide and know exactly what happens in each week, who is involved, and what they'll have at the end.

---

## Title bar

Format: `"Workplan  |  [Action title]"`

The section label is always "Workplan" (bold teal, left). The action title (navy, right of pipe separator) states the overarching approach or tempo.

**Pattern:** `"[Verb] quickly from [phase 1] to [phase 2], with [philosophy]"`

Example from actual deck:
- "Move quickly from mapping to hands-on workshops, with a bias to action to deliver tangible value from Week 1"

**Rules:**
- Must communicate pace and philosophy, not just describe the plan
- Should reference the phases visible in the columns (e.g. mapping → workshops → recommendations)
- One sentence maximum

---

## Column headers (weeks/phases)

- Label each column with the time period: "Week 1", "Week 2", "Week 3"
- If the engagement is longer, use phases instead: "Phase 1: Discovery", "Phase 2: Build"
- Column count should match the engagement duration — don't pad with empty weeks

---

## Workstream row labels

These must match the focus area headings from the objectives slide. The objectives slide names the workstreams; the workplan slide shows them in action.

**Row label colour mapping communicates hierarchy:**
- Navy (`#072E45`) = primary/client-specific workstream (e.g. the client team being served)
- Teal (`#518C94`) = capability building / workshops
- Sand (`#B8AD90`) = technical / automations assessment

Each label can have a sub-label (smaller text below the main label) for specificity:
- Main: "Lyka" / Sub: "People Ops"
- Main: "AI Workshops &" / Sub: "Upskilling"

---

## Cell content (bullet points)

Each cell describes the specific activities for that workstream in that week.

**Rules:**
- 2–4 bullets per cell. More than 4 means the scope for that week is too broad.
- Each bullet starts with a verb: "Map current tools", "Build proof-of-concept", "Present final recommendations"
- Be specific — name the tools, people, and methods: "1:1s with Mary and People Team" not "stakeholder interviews"
- Bullets should progress logically across columns: discovery → design → delivery
- Avoid repeating the same activity in multiple weeks — each cell should show progression

**Verb progression pattern across weeks:**

| Week 1 (discover) | Week 2 (design) | Week 3 (deliver) |
|---|---|---|
| Map, audit, collect, schedule, identify | Co-design, build, prioritise, validate | Present, deliver, document, align |

---

## Ongoing engagement banner

One sentence spanning all columns, describing the continuous activity that runs throughout the engagement.

Example: "Ongoing stakeholder engagement and upskilling of Lyka People team through co-development of all deliverables"

**Rules:**
- Names the client team
- Describes the how (co-development, not just delivery)
- One sentence, no bullet points

---

## Deliverables row

Each cell lists the tangible outputs produced by that week. Prefixed with `✓`.

**Rules:**
- Every deliverable must be a noun phrase (a thing, not an activity): "Current-state tool map" not "Map current tools"
- Deliverables should accumulate — Week 3 should have the most complete set
- Each deliverable should be specific enough to verify: "Automation roadmap with ROI benchmarks" not "Final report"
- Bold text for deliverables (6.5pt) — they're the punchline of the slide

---

## Footnotes and source

- Footnotes for caveats: "Tool preference dependent on client team", "Indicative timing; to be confirmed with leadership"
- Source: "Source: Turing Works engagement hypothesis" or similar
- Separate footnote lines with `|` if combining multiple notes
